FAQ

How does payment work?

We usually pay in two parts: a first installment up front when your project is green-lit—so you can rent gear, lock locations, and shoot. When the film is finished and we’ve signed off on delivery, we release the remainder. For talent, we often pay actors directly out of that closing tranche (and can do the same for other roles we agree on in writing) so people are paid cleanly at the end. Exact splits depend on budget tier and your line items.

Why microbudget? How do you shoot so cheap?

The grant is built for small crews and lean schedules. We plan around locations outside and daytime as much as possible—natural light and simple exteriors keep lighting, power, and location costs down compared with studio sets or heavy night work. We’ll still help you cheat interiors when the story needs them; we just don’t assume them by default.

How long should the short be?

Finished films should run under five minutes (including credits unless we agree otherwise). Tighter runtimes are easier to finish on a microbudget and tend to perform better when people are scrolling.
